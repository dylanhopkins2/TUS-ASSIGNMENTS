Best Time of Year To Travel

Light Pollution


Light pollution makes it difficult to see things in the night sky and the Moon is the brightest thing up there. But bright moonlight is absent from the sky the days before and after a new Moon. A new Moon happens about 12 times a year and dates can be found by looking at the above astronomical events calendar. Those months during autumn and spring are good for stargazing in most parts of the northern hemisphere - the nights are longer than in the summer but not as chilly as they are in the winter! Spring and autumn are also good times to catch a glimpse of other galaxies outside of our own, such as Andromeda. (Yes, really! It may be 2.5 million light years away, but you really can see it glowing in the night sky!) Planet Earth is in the Milky Way galaxy and the summer is a good time to view other parts of it.
